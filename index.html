The Unlucky Leader, the Zero-Point Winner, and the Monday Night Heist: 3 Shocking Truths from Week 4

Another week of fantasy football is in the books, serving as a fresh reminder that projections are just numbers and anything can happen on Sunday (and Monday).

October 01, 2025 By The Shiva Editorial Team

The Beautiful Chaos of Fantasy Football

Every fantasy football week straps you into an emotional rollercoaster, from the Sunday morning optimism to the Monday night despair (or elation). This week was a masterclass in the beautiful chaos of the game, proving that standings, projections, and star power don't always tell the whole story. This week's matchups revealed some surprising and counter-intuitive truths about what it really takes to win. Let's break down the three most impactful takeaways from a wild Week 4..

1. You Can Be First Place and Still Be Unlucky

In a high-scoring affair, the first-place team, Benson and Hedges (3-1), fell to the red-hot Ah Eamon cmon now, 133.17 to 120.97. Not only was this a big win, but it was also the third straight victory for Ah Eamon cmon now in this head-to-head matchup. But digging into the stats reveals a baffling fact: Benson and Hedges has a higher average score in their single loss (120.97) than they do in their three wins (113.17).

This is a perfect illustration of how fantasy football is often a game of weekly variance and pure luck. This is a crucial lesson for any top manager: don't overreact to a single unlucky loss. The process that got Benson and Hedges to first place is still working; this week was simply a case of running into the wrong opponent at the wrong time and falling victim to a scheduling buzzsaw..

2. You Can Win Even When Your Player Lays a Goose Egg

Legs 11 squeezed out a nail-biting victory over The Mile-High Messiah, with a final score of 116.80 to 114.31. What makes this win so remarkable is that Legs 11 managed to secure it despite having a starter, Tyrone Tracy Jr., score exactly zero points.

This scenario highlights the critical importance of team depth. The rest of the Legs 11 roster stepped up to overcome a complete dud from one position, securing a win by the slimmest of margins. To see how crucial that depth was, look no further than "Dicker I Hardly Know Her." They also had a starter post a zero (Jaylen Warren) but suffered a 125.66 to 79.68 blowout loss. This is the difference between a team built on a few stars and a true championship-caliber roster. Depth isn't just about surviving bye weeks; it's about having the resilience to overcome an inevitable in-game disaster.

3. It’s Never Over Until Monday Night

While "Benson and Hedges" rode a monster game from their star running back to first place, their victory came despite a significant roster flaw. In their matchup, both "Benson and Hedges" and their opponent, "Jordan FreeMason," played one starter who scored zero points—a "goose egg."

First, Keon Coal Man mounted a furious rally against TJ Hock Tuah in a true back-and-forth battle. TJ Hock Tuah held a narrow lead after the early Sunday games, only to fall behind after the late afternoon slate. By the end of Sunday night, they had clawed back to a 3.06-point edge. But it wasn't enough, as Keon Coal Man secured the victory thanks to a clutch 14.20-point performance from Garrett Wilson on Monday night. Elsewhere, Josh Retard flat-out stole a win from Tommy Conroy Pedo, with stars like Jordan Love and Amon-Ra St. Brown delivering an incredible 43% of their team's total score in primetime to turn a near-certain loss into a victory. These results capture that universal fantasy experience: refreshing your app after every play, knowing that one last catch or touchdown can change everything.

4. Conclusion: Is It Better to Be Good or Lucky?

This week was a powerful lesson in luck, weekly variance, and the simple truth that a matchup isn't over until the final whistle blows. From the statistically "unlucky" leader to the team that won with a zero, the results prove that anything can happen. As we look ahead, it begs the question: is it better to be consistently good, or just plain lucky on the right week?
